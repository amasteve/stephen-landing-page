Stephen Egar Ayim is committed to advancing inclusive growth and civic engagement in Cross River State. As Ward Rural Development Officer for Ekajuk Ward 1, he plays an active role in community sensitization, policy communication, and grassroots development initiatives that improve the welfare of local residents.

As Treasurer of the National Youth Council of Nigeria (NYCN), Ogoja Chapter, he advocates for youth empowerment, representation, and civic participation across the Northern Senatorial District of Cross River State.

As Founder of Amasteve Integrated Services and Executive Publisher of Ogoja Breaking News, he drives digital innovation, community storytelling, and socio-economic development across the region.

"Bridging the gap between leadership and the people — through effective information dissemination and practical, development-focused advocacy."

Serving the Community

Beyond his professional roles, Stephen is deeply committed to youth development and civic participation in Ogoja. As Treasurer of the National Youth Council of Nigeria (NYCN), Ogoja Chapter, he manages resources and advocates for meaningful youth inclusion in governance and development processes.

His work with the NYCN has positioned him as a credible voice for young people in the Northern Senatorial District — a bridge between youth aspirations and institutional decision-making.

Through consistent community engagement, policy monitoring, and youth-focused advocacy, Stephen continues to demonstrate that grassroots leadership can drive real and lasting change.
